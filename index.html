UK Fintech 新創 · Strategy Lead

夥伴通路策略 0→1：設計一條不需要我也能跑的 B2B2B 成長引擎

背景

公司只有一位業務，直接銷售觸及的商戶有限。但市場上存在一群「一個帳號背後是幾十個商戶」的節點：SaaS 平台、reseller、聚合商、白牌經營者——他們想提供支付服務，但不想自建基礎設施、更不想碰 FCA 合規。同時有三個風險：合作條件散落在口頭承諾裡、夥伴可能亂講受監管的產品（mis-selling）、創辦人是唯一決策者，任何未書面化的承諾都會變成瓶頸。核心問題：如何在幾乎零人力、零預算下，設計一條可規模化、且不把公司帶進合規風險的夥伴成長通路？

我的策略

  • 選 B2B2B 而非加倍直接銷售——一個夥伴等於數十個商戶，獲客成本攤薄，夥伴自帶的信任關係也比冷開發更容易轉換
  • 用「模式二選一」控制風險邊界——掛我方品牌、我方定價、我方承擔爭議的 Connected 模式，對上白牌、夥伴自訂價、夥伴承擔 chargeback 的 Platform 模式；監管責任、費用結構、爭議歸屬在簽約前一次講清楚
  • 用三級架構決定資源投放——以活躍商戶數為門檻，對應不同佣金區間、支援等級與 onboarding 深度；不在早期夥伴身上過度投資，也不怠慢高量夥伴
  • 先 pilot、後放量——以少數信任夥伴做 referral-only 試點，驗證機制再開放

設計的機制

  • 簽約前查核清單——合約只在夥伴書面確認全部費用、模式與爭議責任後才發出，把「簽約後的爭議」轉化成「簽約前的問題」，並留下 CRM 稽核軌跡
  • 十天上線 SLA——首次接觸到 go-live 十個工作天，逐日拆解權責與產出；夥伴端延誤則 SLA 暫停並記錄原因
  • 30 / 90 天 check-in 模板——在摩擦升級成流失之前主動攔截，同時是支援方案的自然銷售點
  • 合規護欄——「不可宣稱清單」、夥伴培訓指南與標準話術：夥伴能賣，但不能亂賣
  • 佣金與誘因結構——依級距設計佣金區間與商戶激勵方案，以競品對標和單位經濟驗算「多少算合理、多少會虧」
  • 兩份文件、兩種受眾——內部 BD Playbook（判斷、話術、紅旗）+ 夥伴營運手冊（設定、費用、FAQ、升級路徑）：同一套機制，兩種語言層次

結果

交付一套完整可執行的夥伴通路體系：兩份 playbook、三級夥伴架構、兩種合作模式、十天 onboarding SLA、簽約前查核與合規護欄。公司從「夥伴合作靠創辦人口頭談」升級為「任何團隊成員都能照 SLA 跑完 onboarding」——創辦人瓶頸從流程中移除，通路擴張不再以監管風險為代價。這套架構同時成為交接資產：我離開後，接手者可直接依文件營運。

※ 佣金與費率等商業條件已模糊化處理。

定價策略 · 商業條件設計

定價對標、服務分級與誘因結構——把「該收多少」變成守得住的答案

背景

早期公司的定價常常是「感覺定的」：不知道自己在市場的哪個位置、不知道哪個訊息最有說服力、也不知道優惠給到多少會虧。我把三個定價問題一次做成有依據的答案。

三件事

  • 核心產品定價對標——逐一對照英國市場主要競品的價格帶，定位自家產品落點；找出最強的價值主張：整合方案的組合價格顯著低於分開購買同等工具，並把它轉譯成一句可防禦的銷售訊息
  • 行銷服務三級分層——入門（只給策略）、核心（內容執行）、完整（品牌成長）三級，每級明確定義交付物與邊界；用 add-on 定價把「可以順便幫我用一下嗎」轉化成有價服務，從結構上解決 scope creep 與工作量失衡
  • 商戶獎勵方案——設計週獎勵、月獎金與里程碑制（非現金 perks 壓低邊際成本），每個門檻都以英國 SME 的實際交易量級距回推「多數商戶搆得著、公司負擔得起」的甜蜜點

方法上的共同點

三個案子共用同一條紀律：先對標市場、再驗算單位經濟、最後把數字轉譯成銷售端能直接使用的語言。定價不是找一個數字，是設計一個守得住的商業條件結構——並且事先列出所有會影響成敗的未決事項（合約長度、提前解約、費率調整機制），逼著決策在上市前發生。

※ 具體金額與費率已模糊化處理。

商務開發 · 重點客戶 · 合作生態

重點客戶提案、合作商會議與聯盟整合行銷

背景

高價值的潛在客戶通常已經在用某個競爭對手的服務，需要一個非常具體的理由才願意切換。我的任務是讓對方在第一次接觸就感覺：「這份提案是為我做的」。

提案的四個構件

  • 情境開場——用客戶自己的規模、痛點與業務型態開頭，立刻表明這不是複製貼上
  • 競品對照——設計成「30 秒看懂差異」的視覺論證，而非文字牆
  • 客製定價——依交易量與商業模式設計，具競爭力也守得住利潤
  • 下一步——一句話的低摩擦 CTA，拆掉所有後續對話的門檻

會議與聯盟

同時代表公司出席重點客戶與合作商會議，並為聯盟夥伴設計整合行銷的營運策略——兼顧夥伴分工、資料歸屬與品牌一致性。提案與會議的品質本身，就是「為什麼信任我們」的答案之一。

產品策略顧問 · 英國法律科技產品

產品策略審查：從「為什麼不直接用免費 AI」開始的定位重整

背景

產品本身做得不錯：工作流乾淨、輸出結構化、聚焦英國法規情境。但網站沒有回答每個潛在用戶心裡的第一個問題——「我為什麼不直接用免費的 ChatGPT 或 Claude？」好產品配上沒有說清楚的定位，轉換就是上不去。

審查的三個層次

  • 定位層——直面「為什麼不用免費 AI」：一般 AI 要你自己知道該問什麼、給的是對話不是可寄出的正式文件、沒有案件紀錄；產品把這些全部打包了，只是沒有明說。建議首頁直接開一個「Why not just use AI？」區塊，誠實回答最大的反對意見
  • 受眾層——首頁同時對個人與企業用戶說話，結果兩邊都覺得「這不是為我做的」；建議明確分流，兩種受眾用兩種付費理由
  • 介面層——風險卡片改依嚴重度排序而非文件順序、行動按鈕上移到風險摘要正下方、信心指標加一行白話解釋、Email 模板加 Formal / Neutral / Brief 語氣切換——每一條都可直接執行

方法

三輪審查各自推進：第一輪打定位與訊息、第二輪設計引導式體驗（上傳後的建議提示、管轄區選擇）、第三輪以真實案例實測並逐項驗收介面細節。好的產品審查不是列缺點，是幫產品把「它其實已經有的優勢」說出來，並附上一份能直接開工的修改清單。

公司 0→1：從零建立整套營運系統，再把它完整交出去

背景

公司在我加入前只做產品開發：沒有營運、沒有行銷、沒有任何系統性文件。我是第一個專職營運與行銷的人，面對的是一個四人團隊：創辦人同時是唯一的全端工程師，他的時間是全公司最稀缺的資源。

我做了什麼

  • 建立跨 Sales、Support、Tech 的資訊流，讓問題在到達創辦人之前，先被整理成「結論 + 選項 + 風險 + 需要決定什麼」
  • 設計 KPI 體系：承諾達成率、延誤提前通知率、重工率、escalation 品質——把「可預測性」量化
  • 以 AI 工作流重設計重複性流程（例如商家資料處理），把邊際成本壓到接近零
  • 建立 Decision Log、Weekly Update、SOP library 等 async 文件文化：每份文件都回答「這替誰省了什麼時間」

交接系統

離任時，我為接手者建立了多章節交接文件、專案追蹤總表（含風險與 next action）、30/60/90 天上手計畫，以及權限分級與新人 Day-1 資訊安全守則。交接的驗收標準只有一條：我不在，系統照樣轉。

Sales Enablement · 知識壓縮

把 12 章上手指南，重設計成 Day-1 可執行的銷售 Playbook

背景

原本的新人教材是 12 個章節的完整指南——知識都在，但新人第一天需要的不是百科全書，是一張能直接上場的地圖。

重新架構

  • 三步通話流程：開場確認與資格判斷 → 價值鉤子與軟性收尾 → 送出註冊與紀錄結果

  • 「Never Say」紅線清單：合規用語、定價授權邊界，一次講清楚
  • 「先升級、不硬推」原則內建於流程——風險判斷不依賴個人經驗

結果

每通電話以結果代碼收尾，資料可追溯、可覆盤；新人不用背熟一本書，照著地圖走就能維持品質。這是我對「文件」的一貫主張：文件的價值不是存檔，是替人省下判斷的時間。

知識管理 · 合規設計

為受監管支付環境設計九層知識管理架構

背景

幾乎每家早期公司都一樣：雲端硬碟從空的開始，大家隨手放檔案，幾個月後沒人知道「最新版」在哪裡。在受監管的支付環境，稽核時找不到文件不只是效率問題，是合規風險。

三個關鍵設計

  • 合規優先，而非便利優先——法務與合規獨立分層，監管文件不與日常檔案混放；稽核進來時直接指向正確資料夾
  • ISO 8601 命名 + 版本控制——所有檔案按「日期_類別_說明_版本」命名，按日期排序即找到最新版；過期版本移入 Archive
  • 存取權限按敏感度設計——個資與金融資料預設唯讀，外部分享需審批；責任鏈在任何時候都清楚

結果

所有新文件都有明確歸屬，沒有人需要問「這要放哪裡」。Company Standards 文件成為所有新人的單一入口——onboarding 不再依賴口耳相傳。我設計時想的不是「現在三個人方便」，而是「20 人規模、監管進場時，這個架構撐不撐得住」。

UK Fintech Startup · Strategy Lead

Partner channel 0→1: designing a B2B2B growth engine that runs without me

Background

With one salesperson, direct sales had a hard ceiling. But the market was full of leverage points — SaaS platforms, resellers, aggregators, white-label operators — each representing dozens of merchants behind a single relationship. They wanted to offer payments without building infrastructure or touching FCA compliance. Three risks stood in the way: commercial terms lived in verbal promises, partners could mis-sell a regulated product, and the founder was the single decision-maker — every undocumented commitment became a bottleneck. The question: how do you design a scalable partner channel, with near-zero headcount and budget, without importing regulatory risk?

My strategy

  • B2B2B over more direct sales — one partner equals dozens of merchants; acquisition cost amortises, and partner trust converts better than cold outreach
  • A binary mode choice as the risk boundary — a Connected mode (our brand, our pricing, we own disputes) versus a Platform mode (white-label, partner pricing, partner owns chargebacks); regulatory position, fees and dispute ownership settled before signature, not after an incident
  • Tiers to allocate resources — three levels gated by active merchant count, mapped to commission ranges, support levels and onboarding depth; no over-investing in early partners, no under-serving high-volume ones
  • Pilot before scale — a referral-only pilot with a handful of trusted partners to validate the mechanics first

The mechanisms

  • Pre-sign checklist — contracts issued only after written partner confirmation of every fee, mode and dispute responsibility; post-contract disputes converted into pre-contract questions, with a CRM audit trail
  • 10-day go-live SLA — first contact to live in ten business days, responsibilities and outputs mapped day by day; partner-side delays pause the clock, with reasons logged
  • 30 / 90-day check-in templates — intercept friction before it becomes churn, and a natural upsell point for the support package
  • Compliance guardrails — a forbidden-claims list, partner training guide and approved scripts: partners can sell, but cannot mis-sell
  • Commission & incentive design — tier-based commission ranges plus merchant reward schemes, stress-tested against competitor benchmarks and unit economics
  • Two documents, two audiences — an internal BD playbook (judgment, scripts, red flags) and a partner-facing operational handbook (setup, fees, FAQ, escalation): one system, two registers

Results

A complete, executable partner channel: two playbooks, a three-tier structure, two partnership modes, a 10-day onboarding SLA, pre-sign controls and compliance guardrails. The company moved from "partnerships live in the founder's head" to "any team member can run onboarding to SLA" — the founder bottleneck removed from the process, and channel growth no longer traded against regulatory exposure. The system doubled as a handover asset: after my departure, my successor could operate it directly from the documents.

Pricing Strategy · Commercial Design

Pricing benchmarks, service tiers and incentive structures — turning "what should we charge?" into a defensible answer

Background

Early-stage pricing is usually set by feel: no one knows where the product sits in the market, which message is most persuasive, or at what point a discount starts losing money. I turned three pricing questions into evidence-based answers.

Three projects

  • Core product benchmarking — mapped the product against the main UK competitors' price bands to locate its position, then isolated the strongest value proposition: the bundled offering costs significantly less than buying equivalent tools separately — and translated it into one defensible sales message
  • Three-tier service structure — an entry tier (strategy only), a core tier (content execution) and a full tier (brand growth), each with explicit deliverables and boundaries; add-on pricing converts "could you quickly just…" into a priced service, solving scope creep structurally rather than personally
  • Merchant reward schemes — weekly rewards, monthly bonuses and milestone perks (non-cash to keep marginal cost low), with every threshold reverse-engineered from real UK SME transaction volumes to find the point that most merchants can reach and the company can afford

The common discipline

All three share one method: benchmark the market first, stress-test the unit economics, then translate the numbers into language the sales side can actually use. Pricing isn't finding a number — it's designing a commercial structure that holds, and forcing the open questions (contract length, early termination, rate-rise mechanics) to be decided before go-to-market, not after.

Product Strategy Advisory · UK Legaltech Product

A product strategy review that starts from "why not just use free AI?"

Background

The product itself was good: a clean case workflow, structured output, a sharp UK-specific focus. But the website never answered the first question in every potential user's head — "why wouldn't I just use ChatGPT or Claude for free?" A good product with an unarticulated position doesn't convert.

Three layers of review

  • Positioning — face the objection head-on: with a general AI tool you have to know what to ask, you get a chat reply rather than a sendable formal letter, and there's no case history; the product packages all of that but never says so. Recommendation: a "Why not just use AI?" section on the homepage that answers the biggest objection honestly
  • Audience — the homepage spoke to individuals and businesses at once, so neither felt it was for them; recommendation: a clear split, because the two audiences have entirely different reasons to pay
  • Interface — risk cards re-ordered by severity instead of document order, action buttons moved up beneath the risk summary, a plain-language line under the confidence indicator, and a Formal / Neutral / Brief tone toggle on the email template — every item directly executable

Method

Each round pushed one level deeper: round one attacked positioning and messaging, round two designed the guided experience (suggested prompts on upload, a jurisdiction selector), round three stress-tested with a real case and audited the interface item by item. A good product review doesn't list flaws — it articulates the advantages the product already has, and hands over a change list the team can start on tomorrow.

Knowledge Management · Compliance Design

A nine-layer knowledge architecture for a regulated payments environment

Background

Almost every early company hits the same wall: the drive starts empty, people drop files wherever seems logical, and months later nobody can find "the latest version." In a regulated payments environment, failing to locate a document during an audit isn't an efficiency problem — it's a compliance risk.

Three design decisions

  • Compliance first, not convenience first — legal and compliance isolated as top layers; when an auditor arrives, you point directly at the right folder
  • ISO 8601 naming + version control — every file named date_category_description_version; sort by date, find the latest; superseded versions move to Archive
  • Access by sensitivity — PII and banking data viewer-only by default; external sharing requires approval; the chain of responsibility is always unambiguous

Outcome

Every new document had a clear home, and a Company Standards document became the single entry point for all new hires — onboarding no longer depended on oral tradition. I designed for the audited, 20-person future state and worked backwards — not for the comfort of the present three.
